The Walk of Growth

Born in Austria in the mid 80s, I was a happy child. Through circumstances and life choices I started distancing myself. By the time I was 5 years old I spent the majority of my time alone in my room - my safe zone.

Come with me on this adventure of personal growth.

  • Get Started

The Safe Zone

Spending the majority of my time in my room meant I would only be coming out for school and to go food hunting. Nights became my favorites. Nobody was awake to stop me from seeking pleasure in eating junk food. Gradually I managed to eat my way up to over 300 lbs.

It's a vicious circle. I ate in order to suppress my feelings, but the more I ate the worse I felt. At school I would see other kids climbing ropes, later at night in bed I would pray for the miracle of becoming lean. All I wanted was a strong body so I could climb those ropes with my bare hand. Phantasies about me showing off in school was what I fell asleep to. Having a hard time making friends and staying connected, I turned to TV and became even more sedentary.

Adaptation

During that time I also developed a passion for computers. The internet allowed me to research and learn what I needed. By the time I was 17 I dropped out of school and started to work for the local government. I jumped between multiple jobs never staying longer than 9 months. All the TV shows and movies I saw made me obsessed with coming to the US. So finally in 2010 I got a tourist visa and went to Los Angeles for 3 months.

  • Read More

The Journey

My time in Los Angeles was inspiring to say the least. When I went back to Austria I had one mission - finding a way to get back to the US. While on the Austrian autobahn I get a call about a job at Salesforce. This started me on a journey of personal growth I still can't fully grasp. The first year and a half I worked my ass off with the single goal of getting transferred to the US. I started volunteering and making great connections. Still I was angry, angry with the world.

  • Read More

Growth Spurt

Having reached my dream and being in San Francisco made me think about myself. Progressively I stopped thinking of myself as the victim and took back authority of my life, my choices, my thoughts. Within two years I lost 100 pounds and can't even describe the development I've made with my mind. I got support to figure out my core values. Got introduced to meditation and mindfulness. Had people teach me the difference between mobility and flexibility. And slowly started building real connections.

Now it is time to double down and walk the path of growth.

Afternoon Walk

A sunny Sunday afternoon in San Francisco inspired me to go for a walk. I started out at my apartment and heading north towards the Marina district and one and a half hours later I was standing at Golden Gate bridge. At that point I was still in good shape so I decided to trek through the Presidio to Golden Gate park and from there I went back to my apartment. A good 12 miles in a little more than three hours. Walking a pace of almost 4 miles an hour made me proud of what I had accomplished. Over the next few days I shared my accomplishment with my friends and while talking about it I realized that I had walked just short of a half marathon.

  • First Challenge

First Challenge

As I fully understood what that meant, my next goal was set. I had to walk at least 13 miles and because I'm a notorious overachiever I set my sight on a 20 mile hike through the Marin Headlands. More than two months later I finally started on the hike. This time I went up to Golden Gate Park, about half way through I made a right turn north towards the Presidio. While walking I made sure to keep Snapchat updated on my milestones. And I was fast - after not even two hours and 6.5 miles I reached Golden Gate Bridge, my second milestone, averaging almost 4 miles per hour. After 11 miles and descending Hawk Hill I took my first break.

  • The Limits

The Limits

Cell service got progressively poorer north of the bridge but luckily I had put the trail on my watch. Never the less I lost my way in the headlands for half an hour. Every step started to wear on me. While the sun started to set, my mind grew more worried and I remember thinking about how I could defend myself against bobcats. Funny enough I was walking on “Bobcat Trail” at that time and I didn't even know. Just before the last bit of daylight faded away I made it to the top and started hiking down “Morning Sun Trail” towards Sausalito. Walking down was starting to be painful. My core muscles started flaring up and every step felt like a knife getting torn out of my lower abs. I barely made it on to the ferry but couldn't get myself to walk back the 1.6 miles to my apartment.

  • Decision & Doubt

Decision & Doubt

The next day I stayed in bed - any movement made me painfully remember the prior day. But I also was flying on cloud nine, 18 miles in 5 hours or 3.6 miles per hour moving average. Walking for the next two weeks was arguably difficult, blisters formed on both of my feet and the blister patches didn't help a bit. Still I thought if I could do 18 miles, I might be able to do more. So I started researching hiking trails and found the Appalachian Trail. 2,160 miles from Georgia to Maine. That was going to be my ultimate feat. It didn't take me long to get excited and even less time to start talking to my friends about it. One night in a bar while talking about it again, a friend brought up the Pacific Crest Trail and the book “Wild” he read a while ago. This of course prompted me to do some research and not only is the PCT closer to San Francisco, it's also a whopping 500 miles longer.

While my excitement about walking the trail rose I knew I had to pace myself. My Green Card process was still not through and that was the singular most important thing to me in the last 5 years. The whole reason I started working for Salesforce in the first place was to get transferred to the US and get my Green Card. So I made myself the promise to walk the PCT in 2017 if I get my Green Card in time.

  • Approval

Approval

Just before Christmas I packed my bags and left for Austria like every year. And like every year we had Christmas at my moms on the 24th, went to the spa on the 25th and the family lunch on the 26th. After lunch I got invited to my cousins for coffee and of course I shared my plans of hiking the PCT. While talking to my cousin Benni that afternoon as well as on New Years Eve, he got so excited for me about the hike. All the love I received from my family and friends during those three weeks made one thing clear to me, it doesn't matter if I get a Green Card or not - everything happens for a reason. That's when I decided I wasn't going to let anything keep me from living my life to the fullest.

I'm going to hike the PCT in 2017 - no matter what.
